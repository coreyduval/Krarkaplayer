Plain-English summary:
Eight coin-flip trials per game are plenty -- going deeper than 8 changes the
measured win rate by under 0.1pp and the average win-turn by ~0.01, so extra
depth only burns CPU. Sample size is the subtle part. Almost every seed wins
100% of the time, but a small fraction of "bad" seeds (a few that essentially
never win) make the seed-to-seed variance large and heavy-tailed. Because those
rare seeds only show up once you sample enough of them, small runs LOOK precise
but actually understate the spread: the sample sd climbed from ~1.2 at 100 seeds
to 8.6 at 800 seeds. Using that realistic sd=8.6, you need about 1142 seeds to
pin the win rate to +/-0.5pp, so 1200 games is the safe minimum (predicted CI
+/-0.49pp). Anything under ~1000 seeds will give over-confident error bars. Net:
--games 1200 --flip-trials 8 (~9600 games, ~12-14 min per sweep) is the reliable
operating point; budget downstream experiments accordingly.
